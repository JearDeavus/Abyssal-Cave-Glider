Abyssal Cave Glider

Noctivagus silentis

Habitat

The Abyssal Cave Glider lives in deep subterranean cave systems with total darkness, low oxygen levels, and scarce nutrients.

Appearance

Pale, translucent body with sensory barbels and reduced eyes.

Respiration

Large, highly folded gills maximize oxygen absorption in low-oxygen water.

Buoyancy

Reduced swim bladder and fatty tissues allow energy-efficient gliding.

Sensory Adaptations

  • Vestigial eyes
  • Enhanced lateral line
  • Electroreception
  • Barbels for sensing

Diet

Consumes small organisms and organic debris; survives long periods without food.
